Step 1: Marinate the Beef: In a bowl, combine the sliced beef with soy sauce, cornstarch, and vegetable oil. Mix well and let it marinate for at least 15-30 minutes.

Step 2: Prepare the Noodles: If using fresh ho fun, rinse them under cold water to separate the noodles. If using dried noodles, cook according to package instructions, drain, and rinse.

Step 3: Heat the Wok: Heat a wok or large skillet over high heat. Add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil and swirl to coat.

Step 4: Stir-Fry the Beef: Add the marinated beef to the hot wok in a single layer. Let it sear for about 1-2 minutes without stirring, then stir-fry until it's cooked through. Remove the beef from the wok and set aside.

Step 5: Cook the Aromatics: In the same wok, add another tablespoon of oil if needed. Add the minced garlic and sliced onion. Stir-fry until fragrant and the onion is translucent.

Step 6: Add the Noodles: Add the ho fun to the wok. Use a spatula to gently separate the noodles and stir-fry for a few minutes until heated through.

Step 7:Combine Ingredients: Return the cooked beef to the wok. Add the chopped green onions, bean sprouts, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and season with salt and pepper. Toss everything together to combine well and heat through.

Step 8:Serve: Once everything is evenly mixed and heated, transfer the gan chao niu he to a serving plate. Enjoy it hot!.

Step 1: Prepare the Cucumbers: Wash the cucumbers thoroughly. Cut off the ends and then slice them in half lengthwise. Use a spoon to scoop out the seeds if you prefer less moisture.

Step 2: Smash the Cucumbers: Place the cucumber halves on a cutting board and use the flat side of a chef's knife to gently smash them. This helps to release their juices and allows the flavors to penetrate better. Cut the smashed cucumbers into bite-sized pieces.

Step 3: Salt the Cucumbers: Transfer the cucumber pieces to a bowl and sprinkle with salt. Let them sit for about 10-15 minutes to draw out excess water. Afterward, drain any liquid that has accumulated.

Step 4: Prepare the Dressing: In a separate bowl, combine the minced garlic, chili oil, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and sugar (if using). Mix well to combine.

Step 5: Combine the Ingredients: Pour the dressing over the cucumbers and toss everything together until the cucumbers are well coated.

Step 6:Garnish: Add chopped green onions and sesame seeds on top for garnish.

Step 7:Serve: Let the salad sit for about 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld, then serve it chilled or at room temperature.

Tips

Adjust the level of chili oil to your spice preference! You can add a splash of soy sauce or a dash of sesame seeds for extra flavor. This salad is best enjoyed fresh, but it can be stored in the refrigerator for a day.
